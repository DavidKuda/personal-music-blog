Inspiring Guitar Records.

Immerse into this inspiring collection of carefully curated music.

get inspired by amazing music,
focused on guitar playing.

On This website you will find music which has inspired me to study guitar. Each week I will post a record which you might like, aswell. Plus I will review online guitar courses I have taken.

Top 5 records

  1. «Wish You Were Here» -- Pink Floyd
  2. «Bold as Love» -- Jimmy Hendrix
  3. «Guitar Band» -- Bob Reynolds
  4. «Hold On» -- Kirk Fletcher
  5. «Wallflowers» -- Jordan Rakei

#1 Pink Floyd: «Wish You Were Here»

Genre: Art Rock

It took me quite some time until I have got into Pink Floyd. And once I got into it, I have found Pink Floyd to be incomparably deep and immersive. Many years before my discovry of Pink Floyd, a friend once has told me: "Pink Floyd is not just music, it is art." I think he could't be more accurate.

#2 Coldplay: «Parachutes»

Genre: Pop

This was Coldplay's first album. The sound to me is very thoughtful and beautiful. The guitar work is very inspiring, consisting mostly of acoustic guitar chords, which are mostly modal, enriched with dreamy tensions.

#3 Jimmy Hendrix: «Bold as Love»

Genre: (Old School) Rock

Hendrix has spent all his time jamming with his guitar. Everywhere he went, he brought his guitar along. He create a unique, distinct style which inspired probably any guitar player after him.

#4 Bob Reynolds «Guitar Band»

Genre: Fusion

This album was a spontenous session. Bob Reynolds had his gig planned, invited two amazing guitar players (Mark Lettieri and Nir Felder) and decided to record without prior preperation. The results are truly amazing.

#5 Kirk Fletcher «Hold on»

Genre: Modern Blues

Fletcher has it all, he has style, soul, depth -- and amazing guitar skills.

#6 Jordan Rakei «Wallflowers»

Genre: Neo Soul

Who thinks modern artist aren't quite that good as established artist should really have a look into Jordan Rakei. He is just awesome!
